About Algeria

Algeria, the largest country in Africa, offers a blend of Mediterranean coastlines, Saharan deserts, and a rich tapestry of cultures spanning thousands of years.

Geography

From the Mediterranean coast to the Sahara desert, Algeria boasts diverse landscapes including mountains, high plateaus, and the Hoggar volcanic range.

History

With a history spanning Phoenicians, Romans, Ottomans, and French colonization, Algeria's heritage is reflected in its architecture and traditions.

Cuisine

Algerian cuisine blends Mediterranean and Saharan influences, featuring couscous, tajines, and flavorful spices like cumin and coriander.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

Coastal areas are best in spring (April-May) and fall (September-October). The Sahara is ideal from October to April.

Visa Requirements

Most visitors need a visa. Check with your local Algerian embassy for current requirements.

Currency

The Algerian Dinar (DZD). Credit cards are accepted in major cities but cash is preferred elsewhere.

Language

Arabic is official, but French is widely spoken. English is less common but growing in tourist areas.
